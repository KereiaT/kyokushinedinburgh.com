Our Sensei

Our dojo is led by Sensei Andras Grof, founder and head instructor of Real Kyokushin Edinburgh.

Andras Grof

Driven by a deep passion for traditional Kyokushin karate, he is dedicated to shaping strong, disciplined individuals and passing on the values and philosophy of this powerful martial art.

Sensei began his Kyokushin journey in 1994 in Baja, Hungary, training under Shihan Róbert Kern. He proudly represented Hungary for many years as part of the national team. In 2011, Sensei moved to Scotland and later founded his own Kyokushin Dojo, where he continues to train and inspire students of all ages.

Sosai Masutatsu Oyama — Founder of Kyokushin

The Kyokushin Way

Kyokushin Karate, founded in 1964 by Sosai Masutatsu Oyama, is more than a fighting style; it is a way of life. Known as “The Strongest Karate,” its philosophy emphasizes rigorous training, self-discipline, and the pursuit of ultimate truth.

Sosai (a title meaning "Founder") Oyama was a martial arts visionary, renowned for his strength, discipline, and determination. His legacy lives on through dojos around the world, where his teachings continue to inspire generations of practitioners.

We embrace this tradition, teaching karate not just as a method of self-defense, but as a path to developing character, respect, and an indomitable spirit. Our dojo is a place to connect with centuries of martial arts heritage.

Kyokushin Dojo Kun

  • We will train our hearts and bodies for a firm unshaken spirit.
  • We will pursue the true meaning of the martial way, so that in time our senses may be alert.
  • With true vigour, we will seek to cultivate a spirit of self-denial.
  • We will observe the rules of courtesy, respect our superiors, and refrain from violence.
  • We will follow our religious principles, and never forget the true virtue of humility.
  • We will look upwards to wisdom and strength, not seeking other desires.
  • All our lives, through the discipline of Karate, we will seek to fulfill the true meaning of the Kyokushin Way.
